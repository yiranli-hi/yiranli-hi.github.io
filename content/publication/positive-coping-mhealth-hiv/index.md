abstract: 'The study examined how positive coping mechanisms mediate mHealth intervention effects on quality of life for people living with HIV. Researchers analyzed data from the Run4Love randomized controlled trial involving 300 participants assigned to either cognitive-behavioral stress management courses with physical activity promotion or a waitlist control group. Measurements occurred at baseline and 3-, 6-, and 9-month intervals. The findings demonstrated that positive coping served as a mediator in the effect of the mHealth intervention on QOL for up to 9 months, with significant indirect effects on quality of life improvement through enhanced coping skills. The direct intervention effect became nonsignificant when accounting for the mediating role of positive coping.'
