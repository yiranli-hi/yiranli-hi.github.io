abstract: 'This secondary analysis examined how stigma and depressive symptoms mediate intervention effects. The study involved 300 people living with HIV experiencing elevated depression symptoms. Participants received either a 3-month WeChat-based intervention combining cognitive-behavioral stress management and physical activity promotion, or printed nutrition guidelines with standard care. Results showed that the intervention had significantly improved participants'' QOL at 9 months, via complete mediating effects of reduced HIV-related stigma at 3 months and decreased depressive symptoms at 6 months. The researchers concluded that addressing both HIV-related stigma and depression are essential mechanisms through which social media interventions improve quality of life outcomes among this population.'
