abstract: 'This research examined how a mobile health intervention called Run4Love reduced depression among people living with HIV. The study analyzed data from 300 participants across four measurement points using latent growth curve modeling. Key findings revealed that enhanced positive coping and reduced HIV-related stigma served as effective treatment mediators. Specifically, the intervention produced significant indirect effects through improved coping skills and decreased stigma experiences, with no direct effect once mediating pathways were included. The authors concluded that positive coping and HIV-related stigma were important mediating factors in the intervention''s success at reducing depressive symptoms in this population.'
