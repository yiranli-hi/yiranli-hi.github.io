abstract: 'The study examined 1,079 HIV patients with confirmed talaromycosis between 2011–2017. Cases and prevalence increased from 125/15.7% in 2011 to 253/18.8% in 2017. Common presentations included fever (85.6%), peripheral lymphadenopathy (72.3%), respiratory symptoms (60.8%), weight loss (49.8%), and skin involvement. Laboratory findings showed severe immunosuppression with median CD4 count of 9 cells/μL. Talaromyces marneffei was isolated from blood cultures in 66.6% and bone marrow in 74.5% of patients. Poor prognostic factors included leucocytosis, thrombocytopenia, elevated liver enzymes, and azole monotherapy use. The research concludes that incidence increased with China''s rising HIV burden.'
