abstract: 'This secondary analysis examined an mHealth intervention using a randomized controlled trial with 150 HIV-positive participants experiencing elevated depressive symptoms. Participants received an adapted cognitive-behavioral stress management (CBSM) course and physical activity promotion on their WeChat social media app. Researchers measured depressive symptoms via PHQ-9 at baseline and months 1, 2, and 3, while tracking engagement through completion rates, item frequency, and time spent. Using latent growth curve models, findings showed a higher completion rate (β at 3 months = −2.184, P = .048) and a greater frequency of items completed (β at 3 months = −0.018, P = .04) were associated with fewer depressive symptoms at 3 months. Time spent showed no significant relationship with outcomes.'
