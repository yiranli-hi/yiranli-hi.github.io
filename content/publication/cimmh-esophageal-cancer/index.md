abstract: 'Patients with esophageal cancer often experience clinically relevant deterioration of quality of life (QOL) after esophagectomy owing to malnutrition, lack of physical exercise, and psychological symptoms. The research evaluated a mobile health intervention involving 20 patients over 12 weeks. Of the 20 patients, 16 (80%) completed the program. Results showed significant improvements by three months, though patients experienced short-term declines one month after surgery. The CIMmH was feasible and safe and demonstrated encouraging efficacy testing with a control group for enhancing recovery after surgery among patients with esophageal cancer in China.'
