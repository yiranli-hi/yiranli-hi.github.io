abstract: 'People living with HIV (PLWH) are twice as likely to have depressive symptoms than the general population, and nearly 1 in 3 PLWH meet the criteria for depression. This study assessed the efficacy of Run4Love, a WeChat-based intervention, among 300 people living with HIV and depression through a randomized controlled trial in China. Participants received either the Run4Love program delivering cognitive behavioral stress management courses and exercise reminders via WeChat, or usual care with a nutrition brochure. At the 3-month primary endpoint, the intervention group showed significantly reduced depression severity (mean difference = −5.77, 95% CI −7.82 to −3.71; P < .001). Improvements sustained through 9-month follow-ups. Secondary outcomes including quality of life, self-efficacy, and perceived stress also improved significantly. No adverse events were reported. The WeChat-based mHealth intervention effectively reduced depressive symptoms in resource-limited settings.'
