abstract: 'People living with HIV and depression have high rates of suicide. Studies of mobile health (mHealth) interventions have shown feasibility, acceptability, and efficacy in improving mental health in people living with HIV and depression. The study examined a WeChat-based intervention called Run4Love using path analysis with data from 300 participants. Results demonstrated the intervention had a direct effect on reducing suicide rate at the 6-month follow-up and indirect effect through reducing perceived stress and depressive symptoms at the 3-month follow-up. The researchers concluded that stress reduction and depression treatment were critical components for suicide prevention in this vulnerable population.'
